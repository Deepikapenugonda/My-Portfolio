**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

This workforce analytics report analyzes 4,410 employees across 3 departments to identify critical HR challenges affecting business performance. The analysis reveals a 16.1% attrition rate, widespread job dissatisfaction affecting 39% of workforce, and serious work environment concerns requiring immediate management attention.



**BUSINESS PROBLEM**

The organization is experiencing high employee turnover, declining job satisfaction, and poor work life balance across departments. 

Senior leadership requires data driven insights to develop targeted retention strategies and improve overall workforce health.



**KEY FINDINGS**



* Finding 1 - Attrition Crisis

Total employees analyzed: 4,410

Employees who left: 711

Overall attrition rate: 16.1%

Industry benchmark: 10-12%

Gap from benchmark: 4-6% above normal

Business impact: High replacement costs, knowledge loss, productivity decline



* Finding 2 - Department wise Attrition

HR Department: 57 left out of 189 = 30.2% attrition rate - CRITICAL

R\&D Department: 453 left out of 2,883 = 15.7% attrition rate - HIGH

Sales Department: 201 left out of 1,338 = 15% attrition rate - MODERATE

Most alarming: HR department losing nearly 1 in 3 employees



* Finding 3 - Job Satisfaction Crisis

Level 1 Very Dissatisfied: 860 employees (19.5%)

Level 2 Dissatisfied: 840 employees (19%)

Level 3 Satisfied: 1,323 employees (30%)

Level 4 Very Satisfied: 1,367 employees (31%)

Total dissatisfied: 1,700 employees = 39% of workforce 



* Finding 4 - Work Life Balance

Level 1 Very Poor: 239 employees (5.4%)

Level 2 Poor: 1,019 employees (23.1%)

Level 3 Good: 2,660 employees (60.3%)

Level 4 Excellent: 454 employees (10.3%)

Total struggling: 1,258 employees = 29% of workforce



* Finding 5 - Work Environment Satisfaction

Level 1 Very Dissatisfied: 845 employees (19.2%)

Level 2 Dissatisfied: 856 employees (19.4%)

Level 3 Satisfied: 1,350 employees (30.6%)

Level 4 Very Satisfied: 1,334 employees (30.3%)

Total unhappy with environment: 1,701 employees = 39% of workforce



**ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS**

Based on the data three root causes are identified:

Root Cause 1 - Compensation Issues

High attrition especially in HR and R\&D suggests employees are leaving for better pay elsewhere

Root Cause 2 - Management Problems

Poor work life balance and environment satisfaction suggests management style issues at department level

Root Cause 3 - Career Growth Gaps

Young professionals leaving indicates lack of clear career progression paths within organization



**RECOMMENDATIONS**

* Recommendation 1 - URGENT: HR Department Intervention

Conduct immediate stay interviews with all HR employees

Review HR team compensation against market benchmarks

Assign dedicated HR Business Partner to address team concerns

Timeline: Immediate - within 2 weeks

Expected impact: Reduce HR attrition from 30% to 15%



* Recommendation 2 - R\&D Retention Program

Launch R\&D specific career development program

Introduce technical skill advancement tracks

Implement project based bonuses and recognition

Timeline: 1 month

Expected impact: Reduce R\&D attrition by 30%



* Recommendation 3 - Work Environment Improvement

Conduct anonymous employee environment survey

Identify specific pain points-workspace, tools, team dynamics

Implement top 3 improvements within 60 days

Timeline: 2 months

Expected impact: Improve environment satisfaction by 20%



* Recommendation 4 - Work Life Balance Policy

Introduce flexible working hours - core hours 10am to 4pm

Implement work from home 2 days per week policy

Launch mental health and wellness program

Timeline: 1 month

Expected impact: Reduce poor work life balance from 29% to 15%



* Recommendation 5 - Job Satisfaction Initiative

Launch quarterly employee recognition program

Create individual development plans for all employees

Introduce manager effectiveness surveys

Timeline: 2 months

Expected impact: Increase satisfaction levels by 25%



**CONCLUSION**

* The organization faces a serious workforce health crisis with 39% job dissatisfaction
* 29% poor work life balance and 16.1% attrition rate 
* Immediate intervention in HR department and systematic improvements in work environment and work life balance policies are critical to stabilizing the workforce and reducing costly employee turnover



**Estimated cost of current attrition:**

711 employees x average replacement cost of ₹50,000 = ₹3.55 Crores annually

Recommends immediate action to prevent further talent loss and protect organizational performance.
